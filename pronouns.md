### **Example Sentences**

Here are some example sentences using these pronouns in context:

#### **Personal Pronouns:**
1. **Ich** habe Hunger.
   - **I** am hungry.
   - **Я** голодний.

2. **Sie** ist meine Lehrerin.
   - **She** is my teacher.
   - **Вона** моя вчителька.

3. **Wir** gehen ins Kino.
   - **We** are going to the cinema.
   - **Ми** йдемо в кіно.

#### **Possessive Pronouns:**
4. **Mein** Auto ist blau.
   - **My** car is blue.
   - **Мій** автомобіль синій.

5. **Ihre** Katze ist süß.
   - **Her** cat is cute.
   - **Її** кіт милий.

6. **Unsere** Wohnung ist groß.
   - **Our** apartment is large.
   - **Наша** квартира велика.

#### **Reflexive Pronouns:**
7. Ich freue **mich** auf die Ferien.
   - I am looking forward to the holidays.
   - Я чекаю на свята з нетерпінням.

8. Sie hat **sich** verletzt.
   - She hurt **herself**.
   - Вона поранила **себе**.

#### **Demonstrative Pronouns:**
9. **Dieser** Mann ist mein Onkel.
   - **This** man is my uncle.
   - **Цей** чоловік – мій дядько.

10. **Jene** Frau arbeitet hier.
    - **That** woman works here.
    - **Та** жінка працює тут.

#### **Relative Pronouns:**
11. Das ist der Mann, **der** mir geholfen hat.
    - This is the man **who** helped me.
    - Це чоловік, **який** допоміг мені.

12. Das Buch, **das** du liest, ist interessant.
    - The book **which** you are reading is interesting.
    - Книга, **яку** ти читаєш, цікава.

#### **Indefinite Pronouns:**
13. **Jemand** hat an der Tür geklopft.
    - **Someone** knocked on the door.
    - **Хтось** постукав у двері.

14. **Niemand** war zu Hause.
    - **No one** was at home.
    - **Ніхто** не був вдома.

15. **Alle** haben das gesehen.
    - **Everyone** saw that.
    - **Всі** це бачили.

### **Additional Notes:**
- **Personal Pronouns:** These pronouns change based on the case (nominative, accusative, dative, etc.) and grammatical gender. For example, "er" (he) becomes "ihn" in the accusative case.
  
  **Example:**
  - **Er** liebt den Film. (**He** loves the movie.)
  - Ich habe **ihn** gestern gesehen. (I saw **him** yesterday.)

- **Possessive Pronouns:** The endings of possessive pronouns change according to the gender and case of the noun they modify.
  
  **Example:**
  - **Mein** Hund (my dog – masculine)
  - **Meine** Katze (my cat – feminine)

- **Reflexive Pronouns:** In German, reflexive pronouns are often used with verbs that describe actions the subject does to themselves.
  
  **Example:**
  - Er wäscht **sich**. (He is washing **himself**.)

- **Demonstrative Pronouns:** These pronouns are used to point out specific items or people and change according to the gender of the noun they refer to.

- **Relative Pronouns:** They connect clauses and refer to nouns mentioned earlier in the sentence.

- **Indefinite Pronouns:** These are used to refer to non-specific people or things.
