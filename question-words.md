### **Additional Tips**

- **Word Order:** In German, interrogative pronouns usually come first in a question sentence.
  
  - **Example:** 
    - **Warum lernst du Deutsch?**
    - **Why are you learning German?**

- **Cases:** Some interrogative words change form based on grammatical case (e.g., Wer, Wen, Wem).

  - **Example:**
    - **Wer** ist hier? (**Who** is here? - Nominative)
    - **Wen** rufst du an? (**Whom** are you calling? - Accusative)
    - **Wem** gibst du das? (**To whom** are you giving that? - Dative)

- **Compound Questions:** You can combine interrogative words for more specific questions.

  - **Example:**
    - **Worüber** möchtest du sprechen? (**About what** would you like to talk?)
  
- **Use with Prepositions:** Some interrogatives pair with prepositions to form specific meanings.

  - **Example:**
    - **Wofür** interessierst du dich? (**For what** are you interested?)
    - **Womit** kann ich dir helfen? (**With what** can I help you?)
